Miscellaneous (0001)

Wednesday, January 22nd 2020, 8:00:00 am math

1. There are several signs + and − on a blackboard. You may erase two signs and write, instead, + if they are equal and − if they are unequal. Show that the last sign on the board does not depend on the order of erasure.

Replace + by 1 and - by -1. Consider the product P of these numbers.

Erasing + and + means P / (1 * 1) * 1, which is P;

erasing - and - means P / (1 * 1) * 1, which is P;

erasing + and - means P / (1 * -1) * -1, which is P.

Clear?

2. Let d(n) be the digital sum of n ∈ N. Solve n + d(n) + d(d(n)) = 2020.

d leaves the remainder on division by 3 unchanged. (Verify it yourself if you are not familiar with it!) Therefore, if the equation has a root, then 0 ≡ 1 (mod 3). A contradiction!
